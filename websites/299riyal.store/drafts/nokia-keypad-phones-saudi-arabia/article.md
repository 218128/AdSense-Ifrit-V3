# Why Nokia Keypad Phones Are Still King in Saudi Arabia

In a world of iPhone 15s and Galaxy S24s, you might be surprised to see shelves at Lulu and Panda stocked with... Nokia phones?
Yes, the humble keypad phone is still a best-seller in Saudi Arabia. Here is why you should probably own one.

## 1. The "Kashta" Savior
When you go camping in the desert, your smartphone battery dies in 8 hours searching for a signal.
A **Nokia 105 (2023)** lasts for **2 weeks** on standby. It is the ultimate safety tool. If your car breaks down in the middle of nowhere, you want a phone that has a battery.

## 2. The Perfect Umrah Companion
During Hajj or Umrah, you don't want to carry an expensive flagship that might get lost, broken, or distract you from worship.
*   **Durability:** Drop it on the marble tiles of the Haram? It bounces.
*   **Focus:** No TikTok, no WhatsApp. Just calls and SMS.

## 3. The Digital Detox
Many Saudis are buying the **Nokia 5710 XpressAudio** as a "Weekend Phone." It has built-in wireless earbuds!
They swap their SIM card on Thursday night to disconnect from work emails and social media stress until Sunday morning.

## Top Picks
### Nokia 105 (4G)
**Price:** 115 SAR
*   **Features:** HD Voice calls (VoLTE), Wireless FM Radio, Torch.
*   **Verdict:** The absolute essential.

### Nokia 6310 (The Classic Reborn)
**Price:** 199 SAR
*   **Features:** Larger curved screen, Snake game, Camera (basic).
*   **Verdict:** For those who want style with their simplicity.

## Conclusion
For less than the price of a burger dinner, a Nokia phone gives you peace of mind. Throw one in your glovebox today. You might thank us later.
